
# A.9. Processing Codes

This section contains the **Processing codes** developed as complementary modules for the **reception, visualization, and analysis of smartphone motion data** within the multimodal humanâ€“machine interaction systems (MMHCI) used throughout the research and artistic works presented in this doctoral thesis.

The sketches included here allow the interpretation of data obtained from the **inertial sensors of mobile devices**, such as the accelerometer, gyroscope, and rotation vector, enabling a spatial and temporal understanding of smartphone movement in real time.

These modules were conceived as **experimental and pedagogical support tools**, intended to:
- Verify the correct operation of smartphone sensors.
- Analyze the dynamic behavior of the device.
- Serve as a foundation for mapping gestural parameters to sound and interaction processes.

---

## Included sensors and modules

The Processing codes in this section cover the following sensors and motion models:

### Accelerometer
- Reading of acceleration data on the X, Y, and Z axes.
- Analysis of displacement, impacts, and orientation changes.
- Use as triggers and modulators for sound events.

### Gyroscope
- Reception of angular rotation data.
- Identification of rotational gestures and orientation variations.
- Complementary data source for motion stability and correction.
